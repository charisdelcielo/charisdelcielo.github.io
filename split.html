$plit
split your delivery fee with your neighbors

Overview

$plit provides a service for college students or any other users in similar finantial or habitual situation to split delivery fee with other users in the same area unit by adding your order to an existing order.

My role

UI/UX Design, Prototyping, User Study

Duration

6mo (2018)
Problem Identified
The problem that I tried to target is the unnecessary hiked up delivery fees that many food company charges. Very often, college students order food and have it delivered due to distance, convenience, or just pure laziness. Also, very few people on college campuses own cars, and thus they tend to use food delivery services frequently, thus providing us with even more opportunity. People that live in a community are less than likely to knock on their neighbors door and ask them to order food together.
Existing Platforms, Exisitng Problems
1. Joyrun - JoyRun tries to do what we do however, they still charge a “runner’s fee” that is still pricey for a delivery fee.
2. Facebook - Some might consider Facebook a potential competitor because it’s a social networking application that includes messaging. However, there are serious privacy and security concerns if people are sharing their location with one another if they do not actually know each other.
Another limitation of Facebook is that people you know on Facebook do not necessarily live close to you, so order aggregation would not be feasible.
High Level Goals

-Design a very detailed user flow for users to use our system to best save their delivery fees
-Design the user experience with which the users can have no privacy concerns regarding personal / location information
User Study
I created three user archetypes for our app and conducted user interviews with potential users who fit in each archetype.

USER ARCHETYPES • Unsatisfied freshmen: Students that live in dorms that are not satisfied with their meal plan that resort to delivering outside food
• Off-campus night-owls: Students that stay up late studying or partying that want a meal at an unusual time
• Broke New-Yorkers: People living a fast-paced lifestyle that don’t have time to drive into the city and don’t have money to spend on unnecessary fees
Design Process
After conducting user research and creating task scenarios, we created prototypes (lo-fi & functional) for three main tasks -- Registration, Start/Add Order (based on user preference), Cancel Order.

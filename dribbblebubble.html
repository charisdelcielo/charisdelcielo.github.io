Dribbble Bubble!

Overview

Dribbble Bubble! is a data visualization project that discovers interesting behavioral patterns of how the viewers' use different reactions (likes, saves, comments) to 3 popular categories (Illustration, UI/UX, Logo).

My role

Data Visualization (D3.js), Prototyping (Sketch), Web Development (HTML/CSS/JS)

Duration

1mo

Motivation

Dribbble is an online community for designers to showcase their work and react to others’. I wanted to observe any interesting patterns in how the viewers react to various posts in different categories.

Curiosity

How does Dribbble users’ uses of likes, saves, and comments differ on posts in different categories of design works (illustration, UI/UX, logo)?

Assumptions

1. UI/UX posts will receive more saves in general than Illustration posts because they have more contents and could be used as refereces by other designers.
2. UI/UX and Logo posts will receive more comments than Illustration posts as other designers can criticize or complement the techniques or the content of the post.
3. Illustration posts will receive more likes than UI/UX or Logo posts as they are more enjoyable and simple.

Design Process

I designed the prototypes for the visualization with Sketch. Our team considered multiple approaches for the visualizations that allows us to compare three attributes at once. One idea was to use a triangle graph that has the three categories as the edges. However, the scaling of three numbers can be challenging as the number of likes are much larger than the other two, and thus the visualization could be misleading.

Simple was the best.

We decided to have a graph with 2 axes with each colored dot representing a post when each of 3 colors corresponds to each category. Moreover, as a fan of visualizations that suggests user involvement, I wanted to add user interactions to the visualization. So, I created a legend with five features that the viewers can play around with.

Result

Despite the obstacles and concerns we had regarding representing the data properly, we still discovered several interesting patterns and observations with our manually collected data such as:

1. UI/UX posts generally received more comments than other two categories.
2. The number of saves for UI/UX posts and Illustration posts are similar. (UI/UX posts do not necessarily get more saves.)

Takeaways

Considering all of technical, aesthetic, and representative aspects of a visualization is quite challenging but is the key to a good visualization.
Also, it is crucial to research about the feasilbility and legality of data collection process in advance.

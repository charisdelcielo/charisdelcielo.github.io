Dribbble Bubble!

My role: Data Visualization (D3.js), Prototyping (Sketch), Web Development (HTML/CSS/JS)
Duration: 1mo
Overview: Dribbble Bubble! is a data visualization project that discovers interesting behavioral patterns of how the viewers' use different reactions (likes, saves, comments) to 500 posts from 3 popular categories (Illustration, UI/UX, Logo).
Motivation: Dribbble is an online community for designers to showcase their work and react to others’. I wanted to observe any interesting patterns in how the viewers react to various posts in different categories.
Question to answer: Are there different patterns in Dribbble users’ uses of likes, saves, and comments on posts in different categories of design work (illustration, UI/UX, logo)?
Assumptions: 1. UI/UX posts will receive more saves in general than Illustration posts because they have more contents and could be used as refereces by other designers. 2. UI/UX and Logo posts will receive more comments than Illustration posts as other designers can criticize or complement the techniques or the content of the post. 3. Illustration posts will receive more likes than UI/UX or Logo posts as they are more enjoyable and simple.
Design Process I designed the prototypes for the visualization with Sketch. Our team considered multiple approaches for the visualizations that allows us to compare three attributes at once. One idea was to use a triangle graph that has the three categories as the edges. However, the scaling of three numbers can be challenging as the number of likes are much larger than the other two, and thus the visualization could be misleading.
Simple is the best
We decided to have a graph with 2 axes with each colored dot representing a post when each of 3 colors corresponds to each category. Moreover, as a fan of visualizations that suggests user involvement, I wanted to add user interactions to the visualization. So, I created a legend with five features that the viewers can play around with.

Takeaways
Considering all of technical, aesthetic, and representative aspects of a visualization is quite challenging. If the type of visualization is expected to desirably represent the data, we face other challenges such as how technically feasible the idea is or visually aesthetic, user-friendly, and story-telling the outcome is.
When working on a data visualization project, we may face realistic or legal issues about collecting and using the data such as the copyright or the provider's policies. Thus, it is crucial to research about the feasilbility of data collection process in advance.
